# coding.kitty — Sponsor Deck Outline

## Goal of the deck

This deck should help `coding.kitty` sell sponsorships as a **developer education brand with a memorable creative format**, not just as a social account with follower counts.

The deck should make three things obvious:

1. **Why coding.kitty is distinctive**
2. **What sponsor inventory exists**
3. **Why a sponsor should trust this audience/channel fit**

---

## Recommended deck structure

### Slide 1 — Cover
**Title:**
`coding.kitty — Sponsor Partnerships`

**Subtitle options:**
- `Coding concepts, explained by cats.`
- `Developer education with a playful, memorable format.`
- `Reach builders, beginners, and dev-curious audiences through short-form educational content.`

**Include:**
- logo / wordmark
- one strong hero visual from the brand
- website + email

---

### Slide 2 — What is coding.kitty?
**Purpose:** explain the brand in one slide.

**Core message:**
`coding.kitty makes coding concepts simple to understand through short-form educational content built around a cat-led visual identity.`

**Bullets:**
- short-form educational content
- technical concepts explained simply
- distinctive cat-based creative identity
- audience spans beginners, aspiring developers, and dev-adjacent learners
- connected to community and event surfaces, not just social feeds

**Proof sentence:**
`This is not just meme content — it is educational content packaged in a format people remember and share.`

---

### Slide 3 — Why the brand works
**Purpose:** show strategic differentiation.

**Points to hit:**
- clear hook: *coding + cats*
- educational value, not just novelty
- repeatable content format
- works across Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, website, Discord
- sponsor integration can feel natural because concepts are already explained through examples and narratives

**Good closer line:**
`The result is a brand that is approachable enough to spread, but useful enough to matter.`

---

### Slide 4 — Audience snapshot
**Purpose:** summarize credibility quickly.

**Use currently known public proof points:**
- Instagram: **173K followers**
- TikTok: **74.8K followers**
- TikTok: **894K likes**
- YouTube: **108 videos**
- Website: **128 videos listed**
- Website: **185 topics**
- Website: **316 cats on the pinboard**

**Framing note:**
Do not overload this slide with too many vanity metrics. Keep it readable.

**Suggested footer:**
`Audience quality matters as much as audience size: coding.kitty attracts people who actively want technical concepts made easier to understand.`

**Surfaces:**
- Instagram reels/posts
- TikTok videos
- YouTube shorts/videos
- `thecodingkitty.com`
- Discord community
- topic voting / suggestion flows

**Key sales message:**
`Sponsors are not limited to a single post. coding.kitty can package attention across content, community, website, and event touchpoints.`

### Slide 6 — What makes coding.kitty sponsorable
**Purpose:** answer “why this channel vs another dev creator?”

**Bullets:**
- memorable brand identity
- educational trust
- multi-platform distribution
- owned web surface
- community extension through Discord
- event extension through hackathon properties
- natural fit for developer tools, security, cloud, infra, and builder-focused products

**Key line:**
`A sponsor is not just buying impressions — they are buying association with a trusted educational format.`

---

### Slide 7 — Best-fit sponsor categories
**Purpose:** pre-qualify prospects.

**Top categories:**
- developer tools
- security tooling
- cloud / devops / infrastructure platforms
- AI tools for developers
- databases / APIs / workflow / backend platforms
- builder ecosystem / hackathon-friendly brands

**Optional “less ideal” note:**
General consumer brands are possible, but best results come from technical or builder-adjacent products with a clear educational angle.

---

### Slide 8 — Integration formats
**Purpose:** make the offer concrete.

**Format 1 — Sponsored explainer**
- one technical topic
- sponsor appears as relevant example / tool / workflow
- short-form execution

**Format 2 — Scenario / story-led video**
- cat-based narrative setup
- technical problem introduced
- sponsor fits naturally into solution layer

**Format 3 — Sponsored mini-series**
- 3–5 videos on a category
- repeated exposure
- stronger campaign framing

**Format 4 — Website + community add-on**
- partner placement on site
- Discord activation
- topic-vote CTA or resource flow

**Format 5 — Hackathon-linked package**
- content + community + workshop + event branding

### Slide 9 — Package tiers
**Purpose:** create productized offers instead of custom chaos.

## Package A — Starter Sponsor
**Best for:** first-time test campaigns

**Deliverables:**
- 1 sponsored short-form explainer
- sponsor disclosure + CTA
- inclusion in campaign reporting snapshot

**Positioning:**
Good for brands testing fit with the audience.

## Package B — Growth Partner
**Best for:** brands wanting stronger recall

**Deliverables:**
- 2–3 sponsored videos
- one pinned website placement during campaign window
- optional Discord/community mention
- campaign recap/report

**Positioning:**
Good for products that need repeated exposure to explain value.

## Package C — Category Partner
**Best for:** brands owning a topic cluster

**Deliverables:**
- 3–5 videos around a theme
- campaign-level framing
- website placement
- Discord touchpoint
- stronger reporting package

**Positioning:**
Good for security, infra, AI dev tools, and platform companies.

## Package D — Ecosystem / Hackathon Partner
**Best for:** brands wanting more than content placement

**Deliverables:**
- sponsor presence on `thecodingkitty.com`
- content placements
- Discord activation
- workshop / AMA / Q&A or event-linked visibility
- optional challenge/prize/judging tie-in

**Positioning:**
Highest-value package because it bundles content, community, and event ecosystem.

### Slide 10 — Pricing logic
**Purpose:** explain how pricing should be built, even if exact numbers vary.

Do **not** price purely by follower count.

Use a blended pricing model:

`Base creative fee + distribution fee + integration complexity fee + usage/ownership fee + community/event add-ons`

#### 1. Base creative fee
Covers:
- concept development
- scripting
- production
- editing
- sponsor integration work

This should exist even for small campaigns because the brand format itself has creative value.

#### 2. Distribution fee
Adjusted by:
- primary platform(s)
- number of deliverables
- expected reach / historical performance tier
- whether content is exclusive or cross-posted

#### 3. Integration complexity fee
Charge more when:
- the sponsor needs technical accuracy review
- the concept is harder to explain naturally
- approvals are multi-round
- brand safety/legal review is heavy

#### 4. Usage / licensing fee
Charge separately if the sponsor wants:
- paid usage rights
- whitelisting / dark posting
- website reuse
- sales collateral reuse
- extended term rights

#### 5. Add-on fee
For:
- website placement
- Discord/community activation
- hackathon/event integration
- workshop / AMA / Q&A
- lead magnet / landing page / resource tie-in

### Internal pricing principle
- short-form post alone = lower tier
- repeated campaign = higher margin and better value
- event/community bundle = premium tier
- usage rights should never be free by default

### Practical pricing framework
Use three internal bands:
- **Test budget** — low-friction pilot package
- **Campaign budget** — repeat exposure package
- **Strategic partner budget** — multi-surface + event/community package

This makes outreach easier when the sponsor has not shared a budget yet.

### Recommended starting prices
These are suggested **starting rates** for outbound pitching right now, based on the current public footprint and the fact that coding.kitty offers more than just a single post. These should be treated as **anchor prices**, with room to move up for stronger-fit sponsors, usage rights, or larger campaigns.

#### Package A — Starter Sponsor
**Suggested price:** **£1,500**

Includes:
- 1 sponsored short-form explainer
- sponsor disclosure + CTA
- basic reporting snapshot

#### Package B — Growth Partner
**Suggested price:** **£3,500**

Includes:
- 2–3 sponsored videos
- pinned website placement during campaign window
- optional Discord/community mention
- campaign recap/report

#### Package C — Category Partner
**Suggested price:** **£6,500**

Includes:
- 3–5 videos around a theme
- campaign framing / category ownership feel
- website placement
- Discord touchpoint
- stronger reporting package

#### Package D — Ecosystem / Hackathon Partner
**Suggested starting price:** **from £5,000**

Typical range:
- **£5,000–£8,500** for standard ecosystem partner packages
- **£8,500+** for broader hackathon / multi-surface / premium partner packages

Includes combinations of:
- sponsor presence on `thecodingkitty.com`
- content placements
- Discord activation
- workshop / AMA / Q&A
- challenge / prize / judging tie-in
- campaign/event visibility across multiple surfaces

### Suggested add-on pricing
Use add-ons to protect margin instead of overstuffing base packages.

- **Paid usage rights:** +30% to +100% of base package depending on term/scope
- **Whitelisting / dark posting rights:** from **£750**
- **Extra website placement outside campaign window:** from **£300**
- **Discord AMA / workshop / live session:** **£750–£1,500**
- **Extra revision round beyond normal scope:** **£250–£500**
- **Lead magnet / custom landing page / resource tie-in:** from **£500**
- **Exclusivity by category/time window:** premium uplift, usually **+20% to +50%**

### Slide 11 — Proof points / reasons to believe
**Purpose:** reduce sponsor hesitation.

Use evidence like:
- consistent brand identity across platforms
- substantial Instagram and TikTok audience
- first-party website with official partners shown
- existing sponsor-linked content activity
- hackathon/community surface expands value beyond pure feed distribution
- strong repeatable topic engine across coding, security, cloud, devops, and tools

**Important:**
If you later get stronger internal metrics, replace public metrics with:
- avg views per post
- top-performing sponsored vs non-sponsored posts
- save/share rates
- click-throughs
- Discord joins
- website visits
- conversions if available

That will make the deck much stronger.

### Slide 13 — How campaigns are kept authentic
**Purpose:** protect trust and frame quality standards.

**Suggested bullets:**
- only partner with products that fit the audience naturally
- maintain clear educational value first
- keep sponsored content aligned with the coding.kitty tone
- prioritize sponsor relevance over volume
- aim for long-term partner fit over random one-off ads

**Strong line:**
`The goal is not to interrupt the audience with ads. The goal is to integrate relevant sponsors into content the audience would already want to watch.`

## What to collect before finalizing the real deck

To strengthen this outline into a sales-ready deck, gather:

- average views by platform
- median views by format
- top 5 posts by reach
- top sponsored post examples
- website traffic snapshots
- Discord member count / activity indicators
- hackathon audience numbers and registration goals
- prior sponsor outcomes / testimonials if available
- sponsor logo permissions / brand assets

---

## Recommended deck tone

The deck should feel:
- creative, but not childish
- playful, but commercially credible
- educational, not hype-driven
- selective, not desperate

Best framing:
`smart, distinctive, high-fit partnerships`

Avoid framing:
`please sponsor us because we have followers`

---

## Working recommendation

The first live version of this deck should probably emphasize **three commercial lanes** clearly:

1. **Content sponsorship**
2. **Website/community add-ons**
3. **Hackathon / ecosystem partnerships**

That will make pricing and packaging much easier than trying to sell everything as one vague sponsorship bucket.
