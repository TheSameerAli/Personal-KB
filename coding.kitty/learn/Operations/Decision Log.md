# Decision Log

## 2026-06-08

Decision:

- The product should target non-technical people, creators, students, and professionals trying to automate their work.
- The core outcome should be understanding coding enough to build and deploy an app using agentic coding.
- The stronger USP is that the learner's own app idea becomes the course.
- The platform should use AI to generate a personalized course map from curated learning blocks, not rely on fully unbounded AI-generated curriculum.
- The model should be freemium.
- The platform should sit under the Coding Kitty brand with some separation.
- The learning experience should be mostly AI-guided.
- The first flagship path should support constrained project archetypes such as personal website, simple productivity app, content/blog app, simple dashboard, and lightweight AI wrapper.
- The product should include an interactive workspace with tasks, code, and progress tracking.
- The long-term business can include courses, community, SaaS learning platform, sponsorship, and marketing-driven education.
- The cat theme should be medium, friendly, and credible.
- Version control, infrastructure, and deployment should be core learning topics.

Rationale:

- The stronger long-term product is not just "vibe coding." It is software creation literacy for the AI age.
- The strongest product differentiation is not a fixed vibe-coding course. It is a personalized idea-to-product learning path.
