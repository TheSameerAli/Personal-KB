# Business Plan

## Business Thesis

AI coding tools are making software creation accessible to non-technical people, but many learners still need structure, confidence, and practical software literacy. coding.kitty/learn can become the trusted beginner-friendly bridge between idea and shipped app.

The sharper business thesis is:

> People do not only want to learn "vibe coding." They want to build their own idea without getting lost. coding.kitty/learn can turn that idea into a personalized course and then monetize the deeper path to a shipped product.

## Revenue Streams

- freemium learning platform
- paid project paths
- personalized course generation
- advanced project archetypes
- templates
- project reviews
- office hours
- cohort programs
- sponsor-backed modules
- hackathon and challenge partnerships
- certificates or badges

## Initial Offer

Free:

- idea intake and simple personalized course map
- one beginner archetype path, likely personal website or simple productivity app
- basic prompt templates and deployment checklist

Paid:

- deeper personalized project paths
- build a simple productivity app with an AI coding agent
- deeper modules on version control, deployment, databases, and automation
- project review and debugging support

## Business Questions

- What pricing should the paid path use?
- Should this be subscription, one-time course purchase, or hybrid?
- Should sponsors subsidize free content?
- What would make a learner upgrade after the free path?
- Is the paid value the generated course, the guided workspace, the review/support layer, or the ability to build more complex app archetypes?
