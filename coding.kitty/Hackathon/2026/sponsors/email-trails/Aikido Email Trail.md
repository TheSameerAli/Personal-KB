Thank you for getting back to me promptly.

  

I believe Aikido would be the perfect fit to be integrated in this sponsorship, as I do know a lot of people within the security space who will also be participating.

  

Please find answers to your questions below:

  

_**1. Would participants be able to use Aikido’s regular freemium offering to run the necessary scans/checks?**_

They should be able to use the Aikido's freemeium plan to run the necessary scans and produce a report. We are not expecting participants to run a full-security audit for their app, but they will get bonus points if they do.

**2. Is the use of Aikido intended to be mandatory within the hackathon workflow, or simply available as an optional sponsor tool?**

We can't make a tool mandatory to use for this Hackathon, but we will push the narrative to the participant as Aikido being the trusted tool, and if they get a clean report, and document necessary actions that Aikido suggested and they took steps to fix those, it'll give them bonus points. We will frame the narative that Aikdio's tool will be used for Judging the security aspects, and the best possible chance for participants to get a high score in that category is to pre-emtively use Aikido and improve their scores before they do their submission.

  

**3. How are sponsors expected to participate in judging, particularly around the “Security Implementation” category?**

We want sponsors to provide a minimum of 1 judge for 2 days to be on the judging panel. The judges will score the projects across six categories, one of them being the Security Implementation. For that category, the judges will be (in case Aikido joins), instructed to look at the security report, and if something seems off, run the project through the Aikido's security scan to see what they score. I welcome your views on this part, if you think there is a better way of doing this.

**4. Do you already have an estimate for expected participant count and number of submitted projects?**

With the initial broadcast and no annoucements of prizes or greater push, we already have around 150+ people on the notify list (i.e. they are waiting for registration to open) and over 200 people have joined our discord server for the Hackathon. We are capping participants to 500, which will be the maximum number of partcipants that will be able to join the hackathon (on a first-come, first-serve basis).

  

**5. Are participants primarily students, startup teams, or engineers from larger companies?**

A diverse range of demographics and backgrounds are taking part in the Hackathon, though students form a significant portion.

  

**6. Will there be a kickoff webinar or sponsor introduction session before the hackathon starts?** 

We will have a kick-off webinar to get everyone together, go through the hackathon, explaining rules, platform and sponsors. Sponsors are free to elect someone from their company to do the introductions and walk through their product during the session, we can provide a 10-minute slot for this. The kick-off will be on the 24th June 2026. Time is still TBD.

  

**7. Regarding Office Hours**

This is still under planning, but ideally, it would be an open channel for anyone to join (capped to max 3 participants at a time) to ask any questions or get quick help. This is optional for sponsors to participate it, but they can if they choose to. For sponsors, we will be publishing a calendar for slots they can take to hold a dedicated knowledge-sharing session or workshop related to their product. This session can then be adviertised to the Hackathon participants and wider coding.kitty community. This will be a chance for Aikido to be hands-on with the participants, demo their product and show how they can integrate this into their projects.

  

**8. For the dedicated sponsor reel/content:**

This would be similar to how the process works curently with Aikido, where we pick an educational topic, and create a video around it integrating Aikido's product. The video is then shared on coding.kitty's Instagram and TikTok channel.

  

**9. Are sponsors expected to provide prizes exclusively in platform credits?**

It is mandatory for sponsors to provide a monetary sponsorship amount as laid our in the proposal. In addition to that, sponsors can optionally choose to also provide prizes or suggest a track where a participants (with potentially a strong security project, but didn't win the Hackathon - can receive sponsor's platform credits).
